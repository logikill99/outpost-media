2026 Regulation Reset

2026 brings the most significant rule change since the hybrid era began in 2014. New chassis, new active aerodynamics, and a new power unit formula with a 50/50 split between internal combustion and electrical energy. Expect the early season to reward teams that adapted fastest — and a long development war through the year.

  • Power unit: 1.6L V6 turbo + far larger battery. Synthetic e-fuels mandatory.
  • Active aero: DRS replaced by an "Override" mode and movable front + rear wings.
  • Smaller, lighter cars: ~30kg lighter, narrower track, shorter wheelbase.
  • 11 teams on the grid: Cadillac joins as F1's first new constructor in a decade.

Purpose-built street-style circuit wrapping Hard Rock Stadium. Three character zones: high-speed sweepers (T1–4), tight stadium infield (T11–16), and the final hairpin complex (T17–19). Tire management across all three compounds is critical in the Florida heat.

Sprint Weekend Format

Miami runs the FIA Sprint format: a single Friday practice, Sprint Qualifying (Friday afternoon), the 19-lap Sprint race (Saturday morning), traditional Qualifying (Saturday afternoon), and the Grand Prix (Sunday). Strategy is compressed — one practice session, no margin for setup error.
